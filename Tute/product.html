Tute's Variant Annotation Software

Tute is a dynamic biomarker and gene discovery tool designed by Kai Wang - the architect of Annovar, a widely used tool for the functional annotation of genetic variants. The Tute platform can be used either from the convenience and cost efficacy of the cloud or as an appliance behind the protection of your own firewall. As whole genome sequencing transforms science and medicine, researchers and laboratories are flooded with high-throughput genetics data sets and are finding themselves unable to analyze this ever-increasing data volume fast enough to infer biological insights and guide decision making in the time required to stay ahead. Tute is the go-to tool for researchers and clinicians to expedite identifying and prioritizing disease variants to aid in the genetic diagnosis of human diseases, all while meeting the needs of the project budget.

Tute novel approach transforms and accelerates data analysis from a simple output list of variants to a full interpretation of sequencing data, and does so in a fraction of the time of other tools. Not only does the Tute perform gene, region and filter based annotation pulling from numerous definition systems and annotations to identify individual variants, it is also capable of conducting multiple sample studies to prioritize the identification of disease genes in a family or population, offering the knowledge of corresponding clinical implications in a summary report. This first-rate output is the result of carefully trained machine learning algorithms and the incorporation of over 60 annotations, more than any other service available.

The Tute Platform

Tute accepts data via an encrypted web interface and employs several methods to interpret this data, using the following approach:

Annotation

Over 60 annotations are incorporated into Tute, more than any other service available. For every mutation in a personal genome, Tute provides simple and intuitive interface to help users determine the functional significance of variants. Tute annotates single nucleotide variants (SNVs) and insertions/deletions (INDELs) for their effects on genes. Tute reports conservation levels such as PhyloP and GERP++ scores. Our annotation process calculates predicted functional importance scores such as SIFT and PolyPhen, and determines if the variant disrupts transcription factor binding sites or microRNA target sites. Tute queries multiple known disease databases (such as ClinVar, private and public data sources) to see if a variant is previously associated with a Mendelian disease. Allele frequencies are also annotated from public databases (such as the 1000 Genomes Project and NHLBI-ESP 6500 exomes).
Advanced Data Filtration for Streamlined Output

Tute allows users to reduce the high volume of sequencing data by adding one or multiple filters to separate out disease-causing mutations from those that are not deleterious. Variant frequency filters, inheritance filters and bio knowledge filters can be applied alone or in combination to reduce the initial high volume of sequencing variants that are discovered.
Machine Intelligence

The Tute platform uses machine-learning algorithms to score all genes in a personal genome by their likelihood of causing particular Mendelian pheno-types. Robust statistical models are utilized that incorporate all currently known information on annotation of genetic variants, resulting in a likelihood score called the TUTE Score. With the capability to utilize both probabilistic modeling and stepwise reduction approaches, Tute ensures that all candidate genes in a sample are recognized.
Association analysis

Tute supports association analysis for variants after annotation to prioritize disease-relevant variants or genes. At the variant level, the statistical significance is calculated by the imbalance of variant frequency between case group and control group. At the gene level, an approach similar to the weighted-sum method is used to jointly analyse all variants in a gene, where the TUTE Scores are summed up and the significance assessed by a permutation test. For family data, Tute is able to perform simple trio and quartet analysis, so that variants are identified as inherited, de novo, compound heterozygous and shared with sibling. This information, along with Tute Score, is then used to pinpoint the disease causal genes.
Genome Management

Swift data querying is provided for real-time retrieval and organization of user files and annotations. Users can upload genome variant files (in VCF and other common formats) via a secure web interface, and obtain comprehensive annotation information from these files.
